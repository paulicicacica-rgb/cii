Car Insurance for Immigrants in Georgia

Georgia allows you to get insured with an ITIN instead of an SSN, and several insurers accept international driver licenses. Your insurance information is protected by federal privacy law and stays completely separate from immigration status.

Quick Answer: Georgia requires all drivers to carry liability insurance, but you do not need an SSN or US driver license to buy it. You can use an ITIN, foreign license, or ID from your home country. Insurance companies in Georgia are NOT immigration enforcement and cannot share your information with ICE.
Important: Georgia does not issue drivers licenses to undocumented immigrants, but you can still legally buy and carry car insurance using your ITIN and international license.
💡 Major insurers in Georgia accept ITIN instead of SSN, so you do not need a US Social Security Number to get covered.

Georgia Minimum Car Insurance Requirements

Georgia law requires every driver to carry liability insurance: $25,000 bodily injury per person, $50,000 per accident, and $25,000 property damage (25/50/25). This is not optional.

Driving without insurance in Georgia means fines up to $1,000, license suspension for up to 3 years, and possible jail time. You must have proof of insurance in your vehicle at all times.

Georgia also has an uninsured motorist database. If you get caught without insurance, it goes on your record and will make future coverage harder to find.

Can Immigrants Get a Driver License in Georgia?

Georgia does NOT allow undocumented immigrants to get a standard drivers license. You must prove lawful presence to obtain a Georgia license.

If you have a valid visa, work permit, DACA, or other lawful status, you can apply at your local Georgia DMV with a passport or national ID, proof of residence, and Social Security Number or ITIN letter from the IRS.

If you are undocumented, you may still be able to get car insurance using an ITIN and an international drivers license from your home country as proof of driving ability.

Best Insurance Options for Immigrants in Georgia

Many major insurers operating in Georgia accept ITIN numbers instead of SSN: Geico, State Farm, Allstate, Progressive, and others. Call directly and ask if they accept ITIN policies.

You do NOT need a Georgia drivers license to buy insurance. An international license, passport, or national ID from your home country is acceptable proof of identity to most insurers.

Get multiple quotes before choosing. Rates vary widely. Do not give your Social Security Number if you do not have one. Use your ITIN instead.

Insurance companies are NOT immigration enforcement. Your insurance information stays private under the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act. Buying insurance will not flag you to ICE.

Tips for Immigrant Drivers in Georgia

Always carry proof of insurance in your vehicle. Georgia police will ask for it during traffic stops. A printed policy declaration page or digital copy on your phone is acceptable.

If you are pulled over and do not have a drivers license, remain calm and polite. Tell the officer you have an international license. Provide your insurance card immediately.

Georgia has no state-specific immigrant resource centers for drivers. Contact local nonprofits or your consulate for legal advice if you face charges related to driving without a license.

Renew your insurance before expiration. Lapsed coverage can result in fines and license suspension even if you do have a valid license.

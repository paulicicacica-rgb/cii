Can You Use an ITIN for Car Insurance? Yes - Here is How to Get One

Many immigrants worry that asking for insurance without an SSN will trigger immigration enforcement. The truth: buying car insurance does not flag you to ICE, and insurance companies are not immigration enforcement. An ITIN is a legitimate way to get insured.

What Is an ITIN and Why Do Immigrants Need One?

An ITIN is an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number issued by the IRS. It is a nine-digit number used for tax purposes even if you do not have a Social Security Number.

If you are undocumented or do not yet have an SSN, an ITIN proves your identity to the IRS and to car insurance companies. Many insurers will accept an ITIN instead of an SSN when you apply for a policy.

Getting an ITIN does NOT put you at risk with immigration authorities. The IRS and insurance companies are separate from ICE and do not share immigration status information.

How Do I Get an ITIN from the IRS?

You apply for an ITIN by submitting Form W-7 to the IRS. You will need to provide proof of identity and proof of U.S. residency, such as a utility bill, lease, or bank statement.

You do not need an SSN or legal immigration status to apply. Visit the official IRS ITIN page for complete instructions and the list of acceptable documents.

Processing usually takes 5 to 7 weeks. You can mail your application or use an authorized ITIN acceptance agent in your area.

Which Car Insurance Companies Accept an ITIN?

Many major insurers accept ITINs, including some that specialize in insuring drivers without SSNs. Examples include Acceptance, Bristol West, and National General. Availability varies by state.

Not every company accepts ITINs, so call ahead or use online quote tools that let you enter an ITIN. Some small or regional insurers may only accept SSN.

Your best option is to get quotes from multiple insurers. Tell the agent upfront that you have an ITIN instead of an SSN. Do not lie or provide false information, as that can cancel your policy.

How Do I Apply for Car Insurance With an ITIN?

When applying online or by phone, select the option to use an ITIN or "other tax ID" instead of an SSN. Enter your nine-digit ITIN number exactly as it appears on your IRS letter.

Be honest about your driving history, license status, and residency. If you have a valid drivers license from your state (including AB60 licenses in California), have it ready to show the agent.

Insurance companies will run your history even with an ITIN. Providing accurate information protects you and ensures your policy is valid if you file a claim.

Step-by-Step Guide

  • 1

    Understand what an ITIN is

    An ITIN is a tax ID number the IRS gives to people who cannot get a Social Security Number. It starts with 9 and looks like a SSN. You use it to file taxes and build financial history.

  • 2

    Apply for an ITIN if you need one

    Go to https://www.irs.gov/individuals/individual-taxpayer-identification-number. Fill out Form W-7 and mail it with required documents. Processing takes 4-6 weeks.

  • 3

    Get a driver license with your ITIN

    Visit your state DMV. Many states accept ITIN as proof of identity for a license. Some states like California and New York have special programs. Check your state DMV website first.

  • 4

    Get car insurance quotes using your ITIN

    Call insurers or go online. When asked for SSN, give your ITIN instead. Major insurers accept ITIN for quotes and policies. Rates vary—get quotes from multiple companies.

  • 5

    Complete the insurance application

    Provide your ITIN, driver license, vehicle info, and address. Be honest on all forms. Insurance companies are NOT immigration enforcement. Your data is private.

  • 6

    Pay and activate your policy

    Choose coverage levels and pay your premium. You can use a US bank account, debit card, or prepaid card. Your policy starts on the date you choose.

  • 7

    Keep your insurance documents safe

    Save your policy number, ID card, and proof of insurance. You need proof when driving and at traffic stops. Keep copies at home and in your car.

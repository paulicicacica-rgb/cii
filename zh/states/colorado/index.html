Colorado Minimum Car Insurance Requirements

Colorado requires all drivers to carry liability insurance: $25,000 bodily injury per person, $50,000 per accident, and $15,000 property damage. Driving without insurance is illegal and can result in fines up to $650, license suspension, and court costs.

Uninsured motorist coverage is also required in Colorado. You must maintain proof of insurance at all times while driving.

Can Immigrants Get a License in Colorado?

Colorado does NOT issue driver licenses to undocumented immigrants. You must have legal immigration status to apply. However, you can use an ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) if you have one.

You will need proof of Colorado residency, identity documents, and your Social Security number or ITIN. Check https://www.dmv.org/co/colorado-drivers-license for current requirements, as rules can change.

Best Insurance Options for Immigrants in Colorado

Many major insurers in Colorado accept ITIN numbers instead of SSN. State Farm, Allstate, GEICO, and Progressive all work with ITIN holders. Rates vary widely, so get quotes from at least three companies before choosing.

If you have a foreign driver license, some insurers will accept it temporarily while you work toward a Colorado license. Always ask about ITIN acceptance when calling.

Tips for Immigrant Drivers in Colorado

Winter driving is serious in Colorado. Snow and ice are common October through April. Make sure your policy includes comprehensive coverage for weather damage, and keep emergency supplies in your car.

Your insurance information is protected by federal law (Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act). Insurance companies do NOT share customer data with ICE. Buying insurance will NOT trigger immigration enforcement action.

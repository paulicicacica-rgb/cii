Illinois Minimum Car Insurance Requirements

Illinois requires all drivers to carry liability insurance: $25,000 bodily injury per person, $50,000 per accident, and $20,000 property damage.

Driving without insurance can result in license suspension, fines up to $1,000, and vehicle impoundment. Getting caught uninsured is serious, but buying insurance does NOT alert immigration authorities.

Insurance companies are NOT immigration enforcement agencies and cannot report you to ICE.

Can Immigrants Get a License in Illinois?

Illinois does NOT currently allow undocumented immigrants to obtain a standard driver license based on residency alone.

However, you can apply for an ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) from the IRS at https://www.irs.gov/individuals/individual-taxpayer-identification-number to use for insurance purposes.

If you have a valid foreign driver license, many Illinois insurers will accept it. Check with your state DMV for current requirements at https://www.cyberdriveillinois.com/.

Best Insurance Options for Immigrants in Illinois

Many major insurers in Illinois accept ITIN numbers instead of SSN. Companies like GEICO, State Farm, and Allstate often work with immigrant drivers.

Rates vary significantly—get quotes from multiple insurers. Consider calling agents directly and asking: "Do you accept ITIN?" This is a normal business question.

Some smaller Illinois insurers specialize in non-standard drivers. Shopping around can save hundreds per year.

Tips for Immigrant Drivers in Illinois

Always keep proof of insurance in your vehicle. Illinois police may stop you, but showing valid insurance protects you legally.

Keep your foreign license and ITIN documents organized and accessible. Illinois has 24/7 roadside assistance and immigrant-friendly legal aid organizations if you need help.

Payment privacy: Your insurance information is protected by the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act. Insurers cannot share your data with immigration authorities without a court order.

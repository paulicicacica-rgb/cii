Rhode Island Minimum Car Insurance Requirements

Rhode Island requires all drivers to carry liability insurance: $25,000 bodily injury per person, $50,000 per accident, and $25,000 property damage.

Driving without insurance can result in license suspension, fines up to $500, and vehicle impoundment. Keep proof of insurance in your vehicle at all times.

Can Immigrants Get a 驾照 in Rhode Island?

Rhode Island does NOT currently issue driver licenses to undocumented immigrants. You must have lawful presence status or valid immigration documents to qualify.

You will need proof of Rhode Island residency, proof of identity, and a Social Security number or ITIN. 联系我们 the Rhode Island DMV for current document requirements and any recent policy changes.

Best Insurance Options for Immigrants in Rhode Island

Many major insurers accept ITIN numbers instead of SSN for Rhode Island drivers. Get quotes from multiple companies - rates vary widely. Some insurers specialize in covering drivers without traditional credit history.

If you have a valid driver license from another country, mention it when getting quotes. Consider SR-22 insurance if you have previous accidents or violations.

Tips for Immigrant Drivers in Rhode Island

Rhode Island is a no-fault insurance state, meaning your own insurance covers your medical bills after an accident regardless of fault. Keep documentation of your residency status separate from your insurance papers.

If stopped by police, your insurance company will NOT report you to immigration authorities. The Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act protects your insurance information as private.

Which major US insurers accept ITIN instead of SSN?

Yes, many large insurers will issue policies using an ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) instead of a Social Security Number. Companies like State Farm, Allstate, Geico, Progressive, and others routinely work with ITIN holders across multiple states.

The key is that insurers are NOT required to ask for SSN — they only need a valid ID number to write your policy. An ITIN works just fine and is completely legal for insurance purposes.

Your immigration status does not prevent you from buying car insurance. Insurance companies are private businesses, not immigration enforcement agencies, and they do not report customer information to ICE.

Do I need an ITIN first, or can I get insurance without one?

If you do not yet have an ITIN, you can apply through the IRS. Visit the official IRS ITIN page to learn the application process and required documents.

Some insurers may accept other forms of ID (passport, foreign driver license, state ID) while you are waiting for your ITIN. Always call ahead to ask what documents each company accepts.

Getting an ITIN does not make you a citizen or give the government tracking information. It is simply a tax ID used by the IRS and other agencies.

How do I apply for car insurance with an ITIN?

Call or visit the insurers website directly. When asked for SSN, tell them you have an ITIN instead. Have your ITIN number ready, plus your state ID or driver license, vehicle information, and driving history.

Many insurers let you quote online by entering your ITIN in the SSN field. If the system rejects it, call their sales line — agents can manually process your application using ITIN.

Be honest about your driving history and any accidents. Lying on an insurance form can void your coverage later, even if you have no SSN.

Is my personal information safe when I give it to an insurance company?

Yes. Insurance companies are bound by the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act, a federal law that protects your financial and personal data. The FTC enforces this privacy law and companies face heavy fines for breaking it.

Insurance data is NOT shared with immigration authorities. Your ITIN, address, and policy details stay between you and the insurance company, unless you give written permission otherwise.

If you are worried, you can contact your state insurance commissioner (called the Department of Insurance in most states) to file a privacy complaint if needed.

Can I Buy Car Insurance With a Mexican Driver License?

Yes. Most major US insurers accept a valid Mexican driver license for car insurance, even if you do not have a Social Security Number or US residency status.

Insurance companies check your driving history and vehicle information, not immigration status. They are not connected to ICE or immigration enforcement.

You will need the license number, issue date, and expiration date. Some insurers may also ask for an ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) instead of an SSN.

How Long Is My Mexican License Valid for Driving in the US?

A valid Mexican license is legally accepted for driving in all 50 US states while you visit or live here, with no time limit on the license itself.

However, each state sets rules for how long you can use a foreign license before you must get a US state license. This varies widely: some states allow up to 12 months, others require conversion within 30 days.

Check your specific state DMV website to confirm the deadline. If you stay longer than your state allows, you cannot legally drive and insurance may not cover accidents.

When Must I Convert My Mexican License to a US License?

The deadline depends on your state and residency status. States like California, New York, and Illinois allow undocumented immigrants to get driver licenses without proving legal status.

If your state does not offer licenses to undocumented people, you can still use your Mexican license temporarily. Check your state DMV rules at NCSL state license guide.

Once you are eligible for a US license, you should convert as soon as possible. Having a current, valid license protects you legally and keeps your insurance active.

Which Insurers Accept Mexican Driver Licenses?

Most major companies accept Mexican licenses: State Farm, Geico, Progressive, Allstate, and others. Smaller regional insurers do too. Get quotes from multiple companies to compare.

Tell the insurer you have a Mexican license during the quote. If one company refuses, move to the next. Do not lie about your license type.

Your rates vary based on driving history, age, vehicle, and location, not on your license country. Get a quote to see actual cost.

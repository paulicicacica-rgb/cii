Can you use a Korean driver license to get car insurance in the US?

Yes. Most US insurers accept valid foreign licenses, including Korean licenses, as proof of driving experience. Your Korean license shows you have passed a driving test and have a clean record in South Korea.

Insurance companies use your foreign license to verify you are a legitimate driver. This is especially helpful if you do not yet have a US state license or Social Security Number.

Many insurers will ask for an International Driving Permit (IDP) along with your Korean license for extra verification. An IDP is issued by South Korean authorities and confirms your license is real.

Do South Korea and US states have reciprocity agreements?

South Korea has formal reciprocity agreements with a small number of US states. These agreements mean your Korean driving record can transfer directly to reduce your insurance rates in those states.

States with reciprocity agreements include California, New York, and a few others. Check with your state DMV or call your local Department of Motor Vehicles to confirm if your state honors Korean licenses.

Even without a reciprocity agreement, your Korean license still counts as valid driving history. Insurance companies will review your record to calculate your rate. Visit NCSL state driver license rules for state-specific details.

How do you prove your Korean driving record to get lower rates?

Contact your insurer and ask them to request a certified driving record from South Korea. You can also request this yourself from the Korean Road Traffic Authority (경찰청) or your local police station in Korea.

Provide your insurer with your Korean license number, full name as it appears on the license, and your date of birth. A certified English translation of your record may be needed—many insurers have preferred translators.

Having a clean record from South Korea (no accidents, no traffic violations) can lower your US insurance premiums. Some insurers offer discounts for good international driving history. Ask specifically about "foreign driving record discounts" when you get a quote.

What do you need besides your Korean license to buy car insurance?

You need a valid Korean driver license and proof of residency in the US (lease, utility bill, bank statement). You do NOT need a Social Security Number—you can use an ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) instead.

Some insurers also ask for an International Driving Permit. This is a translation booklet issued by Korean authorities. Request one from your local police office in South Korea before you leave or through the Korean embassy.

Buying car insurance does not report you to immigration authorities. Insurance companies are not immigration enforcement. Your private data is protected under the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act. Learn more at FTC privacy rules.

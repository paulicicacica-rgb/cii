Can you get car insurance with a Philippine driver license in the US?

Yes. Most US car insurance companies will accept your Philippine license as valid ID for coverage, even if you are not yet a US citizen or permanent resident.

Insurance companies are NOT immigration enforcement agencies. Buying car insurance does not report you to ICE and does not affect your immigration status.

Your insurance information is protected by federal privacy law (Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act), so the company cannot share your data with government immigration officials.

Which US states make it easiest to drive and insure with a foreign license?

California and New York allow you to get a state driver license without proof of legal immigration status. California offers AB 60 licenses; New York offers standard licenses to residents who prove state residency.

Check the National Conference of State Legislatures list of immigrant-friendly states for your location.

Getting a US state license makes insurance easier and cheaper. Many insurers offer lower rates to drivers with US licenses.

How do you convert your Philippine license to a US state license?

Contact your state DMV office directly. Each state has different rules. You will typically need: proof of state residency (utility bill or lease), your Philippine passport or national ID, and a passing written test.

California: Visit California DMV AB 60 page for residency requirements.

New York: Visit New York DMV page to learn proof of residency rules.

What ID do you need to buy car insurance if you have no US license?

Your Philippine driver license is enough. You may also use: Philippine passport, national ID (UMID), or US Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN) if you have one.

If you earn income in the US, get an ITIN from the IRS at IRS.gov ITIN page. An ITIN helps you build US credit and qualify for better insurance rates.

Always give your insurer honest information. Insurance fraud (including lying about your identity) is a crime and will cancel your coverage and raise future rates.

Can you use a Brazilian driver license to buy car insurance in the US?

Yes. Most US insurers will accept your valid Brazilian license as proof of driving history, even if you are not yet a US citizen or do not have an SSN.

Insurance companies are NOT immigration enforcement — they do not report you to ICE or share your immigration status with government agencies.

Many insurers serving Florida and Massachusetts actively insure immigrants with foreign licenses and will issue policies within 24-48 hours.

What do you need to get insured with a Brazilian license?

You will need: valid Brazilian driver license, proof of US residency (lease, utility bill, or bank statement), vehicle registration or VIN, and a US phone number.

If you do not have a Social Security Number, you can use an ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number). Learn more at IRS ITIN information.

Some insurers will quote you without an ITIN — just call and ask. Rates vary, so get quotes from multiple companies.

Which states recognize Brazilian/Mercosur license reciprocity?

The US does not have formal Mercosur reciprocity agreements. However, your Brazilian license is still valid for insurance purposes in all 50 states.

Florida and Massachusetts do not require you to exchange your Brazilian license for a US license to drive legally with a valid passport and Brazilian license.

If you plan to stay long-term, consider getting a US license. Check your state DMV website for eligibility and the documents you will need.

How quickly can you get coverage with a Brazilian license?

Many insurers can provide same-day or next-day quotes online. Simply enter your Brazilian license number and passport number where the form asks for driver license info.

Your insurance data is protected by the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act — insurers cannot share your personal information with immigration agencies. Learn more at FTC privacy protection rules.

After you buy a policy, you are fully legal to drive immediately. No waiting period.

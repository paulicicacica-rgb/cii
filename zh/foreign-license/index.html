Can US car insurance companies accept a foreign driver license?

Yes. Many major insurers will insure you with a valid foreign license from your home country. Companies like State Farm, Geico, Progressive, and Allstate regularly insure drivers with international licenses.

You do not need a US license to buy car insurance. Foreign licenses prove you can drive legally in your home country, and insurers treat them as valid driving records.

However, acceptance varies by company and state. Call ahead before applying so you know if your specific insurer will accept your country of origin license.

How long is a foreign driver license valid for US car insurance?

Most insurers accept foreign licenses that are currently valid in your home country. If your license expires in 2 years, insurers usually cover you for that full time period.

Once your foreign license expires, you must get a US driver license or the insurance company will drop you. This is typically your deadline to switch to a state-issued license.

Some states now allow you to get a driver license without proof of legal immigration status. Check your state DMV website to see your options before your foreign license expires.

What do I do when my foreign license expires?

You have two choices: (1) Get a US driver license from your state DMV, or (2) Renew your foreign license if you can do so from the US online or by mail.

Many states including California, New York, and others now issue driver licenses to all residents regardless of immigration status. Visit NCSL states list to see if your state has this option.

If you get a US license, tell your insurer immediately so they update your policy records. Your rates may change when you switch licenses.

Do insurance companies report drivers to immigration?

No. Insurance companies are not immigration enforcement agencies and do not share your information with ICE. Buying car insurance does not put you at risk of deportation.

Insurance data is protected by the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act, a federal law that keeps customer financial information private. Insurance companies cannot legally share your personal data with government agencies without a court order.

You are safe getting a quote or buying a policy. Many immigrants with foreign licenses successfully insure their vehicles every day without legal problems.

What documents do I need to get quoted with a foreign license?

You will need: your foreign driver license, proof of address (utility bill, lease, bank statement), your vehicle VIN, and driving history if available.

Some insurers may ask for an ITIN (tax ID number) instead of an SSN. You can apply for an ITIN from the IRS even without legal status—visit IRS ITIN page for details.

Do not be afraid to ask insurers what documents they need. Being honest upfront prevents problems later when you file a claim.

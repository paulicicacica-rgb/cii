Which US States Allow Immigrants to Get a Driver License?

Yes — many states issue driver licenses to immigrants regardless of immigration status. This includes California, New York, Illinois, Washington, and about 16 other states.

These states do not ask about immigration status during the license application. Getting a license does NOT report you to ICE or affect your immigration case.

Check the National Conference of State Legislatures list to confirm your state allows licenses for all residents.

What Documents Do You Need Without a US Social Security Number?

You do not need an SSN to get a driver license in immigrant-friendly states. Instead, you can use an ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) from the IRS, or provide other proof of identity and residency.

Common documents include: passport, birth certificate, utility bills, lease agreement, or bank statements showing your name and address.

Visit your state DMV website or call their immigrant services line to confirm exact requirements. Learn about ITINs here.

Do I Need a Valid US Driver License to Buy Car Insurance?

A valid US driver license makes buying insurance easier, but it is not always required. Many insurers will write a policy if you have a valid foreign license plus your ITIN or passport.

However, having a US license removes the hassle and lowers your quote in most cases. Insurers view US licenses as proof you are legally allowed to drive.

Getting a license in your state (if available) is the fastest path to competitive insurance rates and legal protection on the road.

How Does Getting a License Affect Your Car Insurance?

Having a valid US driver license makes insurance cheaper and easier. Insurers can verify your driving record and issue a policy in minutes, with lower rates than if you use a foreign license.

Your insurance company is NOT an immigration agency. They follow the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act, which protects customer privacy. They do not share your data with ICE.

A US license also means you can legally drive in all 50 states. Driving without proper documentation puts you at legal and financial risk if stopped.

What If My State Does Not Issue Licenses to Immigrants?

If your state does not offer licenses to all residents, you can still buy car insurance using a valid foreign driver license plus your ITIN, passport, or US visa.

Rates will be higher and the process slower, but it is legal and safe. Never drive uninsured, even if getting a license feels impossible in your state.

Consider temporary moves to nearby states with immigrant-friendly policies, or contact local immigrant legal aid groups for state-specific options and resources.

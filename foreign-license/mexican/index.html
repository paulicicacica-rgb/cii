Car Insurance with a Mexican Driver's License: State Rules & Insurer List

Many immigrants worry that applying for insurance will trigger immigration issues. The truth: insurance companies are not immigration enforcement, and buying a policy does not report you to ICE.

🇺🇸 All 50 States All Immigration Statuses 🪪 Foreign License Insurance
Quick Answer: You can legally buy car insurance in most US states using a valid Mexican driver's license. Acceptance varies by insurer and state — some require a US license or ITIN within 6–12 months. Check your state DMV for residency rules and conversion deadlines.
Important: Some states require license conversion within 30-60 days of changing residency status — missing this deadline can cancel your insurance.
💡 Call insurers with your Mexican license number and expiration date before applying — most will pre-qualify you in minutes.

Will US car insurers accept your Mexican driver license?

Yes. Most major insurers will insure you with a valid Mexican license as your primary ID. You do not need a US license to buy car insurance.

Companies like State Farm, Allstate, GEICO, and regional carriers regularly insure Mexican license holders. Call ahead to confirm, but this is standard practice in border states and nationwide.

Your Mexican license must be current and valid. Expired licenses may be rejected by some insurers, so check your expiration date before applying.

How long can you legally drive on a Mexican license in your state?

This varies by state. Some states allow Mexican licenses for 1 year, others for up to 8 years, and a few have no formal limit as long as the license is valid.

California, Texas, Arizona, New Mexico, and Florida have the most immigrant-friendly rules. Check your state DMV website for exact rules, as they change and differ by residency status.

Even if your state allows it indefinitely, your insurance company may require conversion sooner. Ask your insurer about their policy.

When must you convert to a US driver license?

If you become a permanent resident or US citizen, most states require you to convert within 30-60 days. Check your state DMV for exact deadlines, as penalties can include insurance cancellation.

Some states like California and New York now issue licenses to undocumented immigrants, removing this pressure. See NCSL states that allow unauthorized immigrant licenses for your state.

If conversion is required, do it early. Waiting until your Mexican license expires creates insurance gaps.

Do you need an ITIN or Social Security Number to insure with a Mexican license?

No. You can use your Mexican identification number (CURP) or an ITIN instead of a Social Security Number. Many insurers accept either.

Getting an ITIN from the IRS takes 2-4 weeks and makes insurance approval faster. You do not need legal status to get one.

Buying insurance does not report you to ICE. Under the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act, insurers cannot share your personal data with immigration agencies.

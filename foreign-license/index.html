Can You Get Car Insurance With a Foreign Driver's License? Yes — Here's How

Many immigrants worry that using a foreign license to buy insurance invites immigration scrutiny. The truth: insurance companies aren't immigration enforcement, and buying a policy doesn't report you to anyone. You can insure your car safely with a foreign license right now.

🇺🇸 All 50 States All Immigration Statuses 🪪 Foreign License Insurance
Quick Answer: Most US car insurers accept valid foreign driver's licenses for coverage, though some require an International Driving Permit (IDP) alongside yours. Validity periods vary by state and insurer—typically 3 months to 1 year—and you'll need to obtain a state license before renewal or your policy may lapse. Check your state's DMV for specific rules on foreign license acceptance.
Important: Driving without insurance is illegal in all US states and can result in fines, license suspension, and vehicle impound.
💡 Many states now issue driver licenses to immigrants without legal status—check your state DMV website before your foreign license expires.

Can you buy car insurance with a foreign driver license?

Yes. Most major US insurers accept valid foreign licenses to buy auto insurance. You do not need a US license to get coverage.

Insurance companies care about your driving history and risk, not your immigration status. They will quote you based on your license, age, and driving record.

Keep your foreign license valid and in good condition. Make a clear photocopy for your insurer records.

Which insurers accept foreign licenses?

Geico, State Farm, Progressive, Allstate, and AAA all accept valid foreign licenses in most states. Call ahead to confirm they insure drivers with your specific license type.

Some regional insurers are stricter. If one company says no, call another. Rates vary, so get quotes from 3-5 companies.

Tell the agent your license is foreign during the quote. Do not hide this. Honest answers prevent claim denials later.

How long is your foreign license valid for insurance?

Your foreign license is valid as long as it does not expire. Most insurers accept licenses valid for 3-10 years depending on your home country.

You must renew your foreign license before it expires if you want to keep coverage. Some insurers will cancel your policy if your license lapses.

Mark your calendar 3 months before expiration. Contact your home country consulate or embassy for renewal info and fees.

What happens when your foreign license expires?

You must either renew your foreign license or get a US driver license. Your insurance will likely cancel if your license expires and is not renewed within 30 days.

The easiest path: get a US license from your state DMV. Many states now issue licenses to immigrants without legal status. Check your state DMV website.

If you cannot get a US license, renew your foreign license through your consulate. Mail processing takes 2-6 weeks. Get renewal documents early.

Do you need an SSN or ITIN to insure a foreign license?

No. You do not need an SSN to buy insurance with a foreign license. Many insurers accept an ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) instead.

Some insurers ask for state ID number or simply use your license number. Be ready to provide your address, phone, and email for the quote.

Your insurance data is protected by federal law and is NOT shared with immigration agencies. Buying insurance does not affect your status.

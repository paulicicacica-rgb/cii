Car Insurance para sa Immigrants sa Florida

Florida ay nangangailangan ng insurance bago magmaneho, ngunit maraming paraan upang makuha ito kahit walang SSN o US license.

Walang connection ang insurance companies sa ICE ligal na bumili ng coverage para protektahan ang iyong sarili.

Quick Answer: Sa Florida, hindi kailangan ng Social Security Number o US driver license para bumili ng car insurance. Maraming companies ang tumatanggap ng ITIN at international licenses. Florida ay isa sa mga estado na nagpapahintulot sa undocumented immigrants na makakuha ng driver license.
Important: Florida does not issue licenses to undocumented immigrants — if you drive without one, you face criminal charges separate from immigration law.
💡 Florida requires PIP insurance (no-fault medical coverage), which is unique — make sure your policy includes this protection.

Florida Minimum Car Insurance Requirements

Florida requires $10,000 personal injury protection (PIP) at-fault coverage at minimum. You also need $10,000 property damage liability per accident.

Driving without insurance in Florida carries fines up to $500, license suspension, and court costs. A second offense within 3 years can result in license revocation.

Getting caught uninsured does NOT trigger immigration checks — insurance companies report to the state DMV only.

Can Immigrants Get a Lisensya in Florida?

No. Florida does NOT issue driver licenses to undocumented immigrants. You must have a valid visa or lawful permanent resident status to qualify.

If you have lawful status, bring your passport, visa, I-94 form, proof of Florida residency (utility bill, lease), and Social Security Number or ITIN letter.

Some immigrants without licenses use their national ID or passport with an SR-22 form to get insured. Ask your insurer if they accept this.

Best Insurance Options for Immigrants in Florida

Major insurers in Florida accepting ITIN numbers include State Farm, Geico, and Progressive. Rates vary — get multiple quotes before deciding.

Some Florida insurers may request your ITIN, driver license number, or passport number. This information is protected under the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act and is NOT shared with immigration enforcement.

If you have no US license history, expect higher rates. Shop around and ask about discounts for safety courses.

Tips for Immigrant Drivers in Florida

Florida has very high uninsured driver rates — carry uninsured motorist coverage to protect yourself in accidents.

If stopped by police, keep your insurance card, ID, and registration in your vehicle at all times. Police stops are NOT immigration enforcement — stay calm and polite.

Many Florida nonprofits offer free legal help and insurance guidance for immigrants. Makipag-ugnayan local immigrant advocacy groups in your county.

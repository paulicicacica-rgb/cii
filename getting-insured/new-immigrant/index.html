Car Insurance for New Immigrants: What to Do the Day You Arrive

Starting from zero in a new country is overwhelming and car insurance can feel like one more barrier. Insurers want your business and buying a policy does not report you to immigration authorities.

🇺🇸 All 50 States All Immigration Statuses 🚗 Getting Insured
Quick Answer: New immigrants can legally buy car insurance in the US, even without a Social Security Number or US driver license. Most insurers accept a foreign license, passport, or ITIN as ID. Requirements vary by state so check your state DMV for local rules.
Important: Driving without insurance is illegal in nearly every US state and can result in fines, license suspension, or vehicle impoundment — get covered before you drive.
💡 If you do not have an SSN yet, ask insurers specifically if they accept a passport or ITIN as an alternative identifier — many do.

What Do You Need to Get Car Insurance on Day 1 as a New Immigrant?

You can get car insurance in the US even without a Social Security Number, a US license, or permanent residency status.

On day 1, you need: your passport, your foreign or international driver license, and a US address where you are staying.

Some insurers also accept an ITIN instead of an SSN. You can learn about ITINs at the IRS official site.

Rates vary by insurer and state — get quotes from multiple companies to find what works for your situation.

Will Getting Car Insurance Put Your Immigration Status at Risk?

No. Insurance companies are private businesses — they are not immigration enforcement and they do not report customers to ICE.

Your personal data is protected by federal law. The Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act limits how insurers share your information. See details at the FTC official site.

Buying car insurance does not trigger any immigration consequences. You are simply purchasing a legal financial product.

The National Association of Insurance Commissioners oversees insurer conduct. Learn more at NAIC.org.

Do You Need a US Driver License Before You Can Get Insured?

No — many insurers will cover you using your valid foreign driver license, especially in your first weeks in the US.

However, you will need a US state license soon. Over 19 states allow undocumented immigrants to get a driver license. Check the full list at the NCSL official site.

California issues AB 60 licenses regardless of immigration status. Details are at the CA DMV site.

License eligibility rules vary by state — check your state DMV if you are unsure what applies where you live.

What Should You Do in Your First Month to Stay Properly Insured?

Week 1: Get at least a basic liability policy so you are legal to drive. Liability coverage is required in almost every US state.

Week 2: Apply for your state driver license as soon as possible. Having a US license often lowers your insurance rate over time.

Month 1: Review your policy and add coverage like collision or comprehensive if you own or are financing a vehicle.

Keep your insurance card in your vehicle at all times — you are required to show proof of insurance during traffic stops in all states.

Step-by-Step Guide

  • 1

    Check your state driving license rules

    Some states issue licenses to immigrants without SSN. Visit your state DMV website or check ncsl.org to see what documents you need.

  • 2

    Gather your ID documents

    Collect your passport, foreign license, visa, ITIN, or matricula consular. Insurers accept various IDs — an SSN is not always required.

  • 3

    Find immigrant-friendly insurers

    Some major insurers accept ITINs or foreign licenses. Call ahead and ask. Do not pay without confirming they can cover your situation.

  • 4

    Get your driving history translated

    A foreign driving record can lower your rates. Ask your home country motor authority for an official record, then get a certified translation.

  • 5

    Compare quotes from multiple companies

    Rates vary widely. Get at least three quotes. Ask each insurer exactly which documents they need before sharing personal information.

  • 6

    Understand your policy before you sign

    Ask what is covered, what your deductible is, and when coverage starts. Do not drive until you have proof of insurance in hand.

  • 7

    Know your privacy rights

    Insurance companies are not immigration enforcement. The Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act protects your data. Buying insurance does not notify ICE.

Car Insurance for New Immigrants: What You Need From Day One

Buying car insurance when you are new to the US feels overwhelming, especially if you are worried about your status. Insurance companies are not immigration enforcement and cannot report you to ICE.

🇺🇸 All 50 States All Immigration Statuses 🚗 Getting Insured
Quick Answer: You can get car insurance in the US even if you just arrived, have no SSN, and hold only a foreign license. Insurers are regulated by state law, not immigration law. Your privacy is protected under the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act.
Important: Driving without insurance is illegal in nearly every US state and can result in fines, license suspension, or vehicle impoundment — get at least minimum liability coverage before driving.
💡 If you do not yet have a US license, ask insurers specifically whether they accept a foreign license — many do, and it can get you covered on day one.

What Do You Need to Buy Car Insurance on Day 1 in the US?

You can buy car insurance the same day you arrive — you do not need a US license or SSN to get started.

Bring your foreign passport, your foreign driver license, and your address in the US (even a temporary one works).

Some insurers also accept an ITIN instead of an SSN. You can apply for an ITIN at IRS.gov.

Insurance companies are not immigration enforcement. Buying a policy does not report you to ICE or any federal agency.

Can You Get Insured Without a US Driver License in Week 1?

Yes — most major insurers will accept a valid foreign driver license to open a policy while you wait for a US license.

Over 19 states allow undocumented immigrants to obtain a state driver license. Check the full list at NCSL.org.

In California, the AB60 license is available regardless of immigration status. Details at CA DMV.

Getting a state license in your first weeks makes getting insured faster and often lowers your rate. Rates vary — get a quote.

What Insurance Coverage Do New Immigrants Actually Need?

Every state except New Hampshire requires at least liability insurance. This covers damage you cause to other people and their property.

If you are financing or leasing a car, your lender will require comprehensive and collision coverage as well.

New immigrants who are unfamiliar with US roads may benefit from uninsured motorist coverage — it protects you if the other driver has no insurance.

For guidance on standard coverage types, the National Association of Insurance Commissioners has free consumer resources in multiple languages.

Is Your Personal Information Safe When You Buy Car Insurance as an Immigrant?

Yes. Insurance companies are legally required to protect your personal data under the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act. Learn more at FTC.gov.

Insurers cannot legally share your information with immigration authorities without a court order. Your application data stays private.

Do not let fear of data sharing stop you from getting covered. Driving uninsured carries serious legal and financial risk in every US state.

Your immigration status does not disqualify you from buying car insurance. Focus on getting legal minimum coverage first, then improve your policy over time.

Step-by-Step Guide

  • 1

    Check if your state requires a license

    Most states require a valid license to buy insurance. Over 20 states issue licenses to undocumented immigrants. Check your state DMV first.

  • 2

    Get your driver license or permit

    Use a foreign license, apply for a state license, or get an ITIN. Some states accept foreign licenses directly for insurance purposes.

  • 3

    Gather your documents

    Collect your passport, visa or ITIN, foreign driving record if available, and your US address. More documents can mean better rates.

  • 4

    Request quotes from multiple insurers

    Contact at least 3 insurers. Some specialize in new immigrants. Rates vary widely so comparing quotes saves you real money.

  • 5

    Know your privacy rights

    Insurers cannot share your data with ICE. The Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act protects your information. Buying insurance is private and safe.

  • 6

    Choose your coverage level

    Every state has minimum liability requirements. If you have a car loan, lenders require full coverage. Ask your insurer what your state requires.

  • 7

    Activate your policy and carry proof

    Once you pay, save your insurance card on your phone and in your car. You must show proof of insurance if stopped by police.

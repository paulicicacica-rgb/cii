How to Get Car Insurance Without a Social Security Number

Many immigrants avoid buying insurance fearing it will expose their status — that fear is understandable but unfounded. Insurance companies are not immigration enforcement, and your data is protected by federal privacy law.

🇺🇸 All 50 States All Immigration Statuses 🚗 Getting Insured
Quick Answer: You can legally get car insurance without an SSN by using an ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) instead. Many major insurers accept ITINs as a valid form of identification. Your immigration status does not disqualify you from buying car insurance.
Important: Never drive without insurance — in every US state, driving uninsured can result in fines, license suspension, and vehicle impoundment regardless of immigration status.
💡 You do not need an SSN to get car insurance — an ITIN, foreign passport, or state-issued license is accepted by many major insurers across the US.

Can You Get Car Insurance Without a Social Security Number?

Yes — you can get car insurance without an SSN. Many major insurers accept an ITIN or foreign passport as identification instead.

Insurance companies are not immigration enforcement. They do not report you to ICE or share your data with federal agencies.

Your information is protected under the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act. See details at FTC.gov.

What Is an ITIN and How Do You Use It for Insurance?

An ITIN is a 9-digit tax ID issued by the IRS to people who do not have an SSN. It starts with the number 9.

Many insurers accept an ITIN in the SSN field on an application. It is a legitimate government-issued number for this purpose.

You can apply for an ITIN through the IRS. Visit IRS.gov ITIN page for the official application process.

If you do not have an ITIN yet, some insurers will still quote you using a passport number or foreign license number.

Which Documents Do You Need to Get Insured Without an SSN?

Bring as many of these as possible: ITIN letter, foreign or US driver license, passport, vehicle title, and proof of address.

If your state issues licenses to undocumented residents, that license works for insurance. Check the full list at NCSL.org.

California AB 60 licenses are accepted by insurers. See details at CA DMV AB60 page.

Having more documents ready speeds up same-day coverage. Call the insurer directly and ask which ID they accept before applying.

How Do You Get Covered the Same Day Without an SSN?

Call insurers directly rather than applying online — phone agents have more flexibility to process non-SSN applications same day.

Ask specifically: "Do you accept ITIN in place of SSN?" Insurers that often say yes include Progressive, GEICO, and State Farm — but policies vary by state and agent.

Once approved, you receive a declarations page and ID cards immediately by email. You are legally insured from that moment.

Rates vary based on your driving record and location. Get multiple quotes to find the best price for your situation.

Step-by-Step Guide

  • 1

    Gather your available ID documents

    Collect your passport, foreign driver license, visa, ITIN, or state ID. Insurers use these instead of an SSN to verify your identity.

  • 2

    Get an ITIN if you do not have one

    An ITIN from the IRS can replace an SSN for many insurers. Apply at irs.gov. It is safe and does not affect your immigration status.

  • 3

    Find insurers that accept no-SSN applicants

    Many major insurers and local agents insure drivers without an SSN. Search for companies that list ITIN or passport as accepted ID options.

  • 4

    Request quotes using your alternative ID

    Give each insurer your ITIN or passport number instead of an SSN. Rates vary, so get at least three quotes before choosing a policy.

  • 5

    Provide your foreign or state driver license

    Submit your valid foreign license or US state license. Some states issue licenses regardless of immigration status — check your state DMV.

  • 6

    Review and select your coverage

    Choose at minimum the liability coverage required in your state. Higher coverage protects you more if you are in a serious accident.

  • 7

    Know your data is legally protected

    Insurers cannot share your personal data with ICE. The Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act legally protects your insurance customer information.

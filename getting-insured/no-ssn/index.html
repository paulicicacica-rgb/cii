How to Get Car Insurance Without a Social Security Number

Worried that applying for insurance without an SSN will cause problems with immigration? Insurance companies are not immigration enforcement — they cannot and do not report you to ICE.

🇺🇸 All 50 States All Immigration Statuses 🚗 Getting Insured
Quick Answer: You can legally get car insurance without an SSN by using an ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) issued by the IRS. No federal law requires insurers to collect your SSN. Many major carriers accept an ITIN as a valid alternative identifier.
Important: Never give an insurer a fake SSN — this is fraud and can result in your policy being canceled and criminal charges filed against you.
💡 If you do not yet have an ITIN, you can still get insured — ask insurers that accept a passport or foreign consular ID as your primary identifier.

Can You Get Car Insurance Without a Social Security Number?

Yes — you can get car insurance without an SSN. Many major insurers accept an ITIN or foreign ID instead.

Insurance companies are private businesses. They are not immigration enforcement and do not report you to ICE or any government agency.

Your data is protected by federal law. The Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act requires insurers to keep your personal information private.

What Is an ITIN and How Do You Use It for Insurance?

An ITIN is a tax ID number issued by the IRS to people who do not have an SSN. It is nine digits and starts with the number 9.

You can apply for an ITIN through the IRS. Visit the official page: IRS ITIN Information.

When applying for insurance, give your ITIN where the form asks for an SSN. Many insurers accept it without issue.

If an insurer refuses your ITIN, move on — other insurers will work with you. Rates vary, so get multiple quotes.

Which Documents Do You Need to Get Insured Without an SSN?

Bring your ITIN letter from the IRS, a valid photo ID such as a passport or state ID, and your vehicle registration.

If your state issues licenses to undocumented immigrants, use that license. Check the state list at NCSL State License Laws.

California residents can use an AB 60 license. Details are at the CA DMV AB 60 page.

A foreign passport or consular ID is accepted by many insurers when no US license exists. Always ask before assuming you cannot qualify.

How Do You Get Covered the Same Day Without an SSN?

Call an insurer directly and tell them you have an ITIN. Same-day coverage is possible once you pay your first premium.

Progressive, State Farm, and GEICO have accepted ITINs in many states — but policies vary by location. Always confirm before applying.

Independent insurance agents who serve immigrant communities can help you find coverage fast. They know which local insurers accept ITINs.

For complaints or questions about insurer practices, contact your state regulator through NAIC.org.

Step-by-Step Guide

  • 1

    Gather your accepted ID documents

    Collect your foreign passport, visa, ITIN, foreign or state-issued license. Insurers accept these instead of an SSN.

  • 2

    Find insurers that do not require SSN

    Contact insurers directly and ask if they write policies without an SSN. Many major and regional carriers allow this.

  • 3

    Get your ITIN if you have one

    An IRS ITIN can substitute for an SSN at many insurers. Apply at irs.gov/itin if you do not yet have one.

  • 4

    Compare quotes from multiple insurers

    Rates vary widely. Get at least three quotes using your ITIN or passport number so you can compare real costs.

  • 5

    Provide your driving history

    Bring foreign driving records or an international license. Some insurers accept overseas history to lower your rate.

  • 6

    Confirm your privacy is protected

    Insurers cannot share your data with ICE. The Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act legally protects your insurance information.

  • 7

    Purchase and keep proof of insurance

    Once approved, save your insurance ID card digitally and in your vehicle. You are legally required to carry proof.

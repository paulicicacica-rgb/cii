Car Insurance With No US Credit History: What Every Immigrant Needs to Know

Many immigrants worry that having no US credit history means they cannot get insured. You can — and your immigration status does not affect your legal right to buy car insurance.

Quick Answer: Most US insurers use your credit score to set rates, but no credit history is not the same as bad credit. Several insurers offer policies without a US credit check. Rates vary — get a quote.
Important: Never provide false information to an insurer about your identity or driving history — doing so is fraud and can void your coverage.
💡 Ask each insurer directly whether they use credit scores for rating in your state — some will waive the requirement for new arrivals.

Does No US Credit History Mean You Cannot Get Car Insurance?

You can get car insurance in the US even with zero credit history. Many insurers will still cover you.

Most states allow insurers to use a "credit-based insurance score" to set rates. No credit means no score — not a bad score.

Some insurers specialize in drivers with no US credit history, including new immigrants. Rates vary, so get a quote from multiple companies.

Insurance companies are not immigration enforcement. Buying a policy does not trigger any report to ICE or any federal agency.

Which Insurers Look Past Your Lack of US Credit History?

Several major insurers offer policies without requiring a US credit score. Options vary by state, so always compare quotes.

Look for insurers that accept an ITIN instead of an SSN. An ITIN is issued by the IRS and is valid ID for financial purposes. Learn more at IRS.gov ITIN page.

Some insurers weigh your foreign driving record, years of driving experience, or home-country license as positive factors.

The NAIC offers a free consumer guide to understanding insurance scores at NAIC.org.

How Does No Credit History Actually Affect Your Premium?

Without a US credit history, some insurers place you in a higher-risk category. This can raise your quoted rate.

This is not permanent. As you build US credit, your insurance score improves and your premium can go down at renewal.

California, Hawaii, Massachusetts, and Michigan do not allow insurers to use credit scores for auto insurance pricing. Check your state DMV for local rules.

Your driving record, vehicle type, and coverage level all affect your rate as much as or more than your credit history.

How Can You Build US Credit to Lower Your Car Insurance Rate?

Opening a secured credit card or a credit-builder loan are two of the fastest ways to start a US credit history as a new immigrant.

Paying every bill on time — phone, utilities, credit card — builds your credit score steadily over 6 to 12 months.

Some states allow undocumented immigrants to obtain a state driver license, which also helps establish a financial footprint. See the full list at NCSL.org.

Your insurance data is protected by the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act. Learn more at FTC.gov.

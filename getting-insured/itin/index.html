How to Get Car Insurance With an ITIN Number (No SSN Needed)

Worried that applying for insurance without an SSN will cause problems? Insurers are not immigration enforcement and your data is federally protected under the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act.

Important: Never purchase insurance from an unlicensed seller — always verify any insurer is licensed in your state before paying for a policy.
💡 When you call an insurer, simply say you have an ITIN instead of an SSN — most agents handle this regularly and it is a straightforward process.

Can You Get Car Insurance With an ITIN Number?

Yes — many major insurers accept an ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) instead of a Social Security Number to open a policy.

An ITIN is issued by the IRS for tax purposes. It is a 9-digit number starting with the digit 9. It is not a work permit or immigration document.

Using your ITIN for insurance is legal and common. It does not trigger any immigration enforcement action. Insurers are not immigration agencies.

Learn more about ITINs at the official IRS ITIN page.

Which Major Insurers Accept an ITIN for Car Insurance?

Several well-known insurers are known to accept ITINs, though policies vary by state and agent. Always confirm directly when you call or apply online.

Progressive, State Farm, and Geico have accepted ITINs in many states. Some regional and non-standard insurers specialize in coverage for immigrants and may be more flexible.

Requirements vary. Some insurers accept an ITIN plus a foreign license. Others require a US license. Rates vary — get a quote to find your actual cost.

The National Association of Insurance Commissioners (NAIC) can help you verify that any insurer is licensed in your state.

Is Your Personal Information Safe When You Use an ITIN?

Yes. Insurance companies are legally required to protect your personal data. They are not permitted to share it with immigration authorities.

The Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act requires insurers to keep your financial and personal information private and secure.

Buying a car insurance policy does not give ICE or any government agency access to your information. Your ITIN is used for identification only.

You can safely provide your ITIN, address, and vehicle information when applying for coverage without fear of immigration consequences.

What Else Do You Need Besides an ITIN to Get Insured?

Most insurers will also ask for your driver license, vehicle registration, and basic vehicle information when you apply for a policy.

If you do not have a US license, some states allow undocumented immigrants to obtain a state license. Check the NCSL list of states that issue licenses to undocumented immigrants.

California residents can apply for an AB 60 license. See the California DMV AB 60 fast facts for full details.

Some insurers also accept a foreign passport or consular ID as secondary identification alongside your ITIN. Requirements vary by state and insurer.

Step-by-Step Guide

  • 1

    Get Your ITIN From the IRS

    Apply for an ITIN at irs.gov if you do not have one. This number replaces an SSN for tax and financial purposes, including some insurance applications.

  • 2

    Find ITIN-Friendly Insurers

    Not all insurers accept ITINs, but many do. Call insurers directly and ask if they accept an ITIN instead of an SSN when applying for a policy.

  • 3

    Get a Valid Driver License

    Over 19 states allow undocumented immigrants to get a license. Check ncsl.org for your state. A valid license is required before any insurer will cover you.

  • 4

    Gather Your Documents

    Have your ITIN letter, driver license, vehicle title or registration, and proof of address ready before contacting insurers or requesting quotes.

  • 5

    Request Quotes From Multiple Insurers

    Rates vary widely. Contact at least three insurers that accept ITINs and compare coverage options. Do not accept the first offer without comparing others.

  • 6

    Choose Coverage and Buy Your Policy

    Select at minimum your state required liability coverage. Ask the insurer to send proof of insurance immediately so you can carry it while driving.

  • 7

    Know Your Privacy Rights

    Insurers cannot share your data with ICE. The Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act protects your information. Buying insurance will not put your immigration status at risk.

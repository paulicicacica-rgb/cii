How to Get Car Insurance Using Your ITIN Number

Many immigrants worry that applying for car insurance without an SSN will cause problems - it will not. Your ITIN is a widely accepted ID, and insurers are not immigration enforcement.

🇺🇸 All 50 States All Immigration Statuses 🚗 Getting Insured
Quick Answer: An ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is a tax ID issued by the IRS to people who do not qualify for a Social Security Number. Many major insurers accept it as valid identification when applying for coverage. Learn more at https://www.irs.gov/individuals/individual-taxpayer-identification-number
Important: Do not assume an insurer accepts ITINs — always confirm directly with the company or agent before submitting your application, as policies differ by state and insurer.
💡 Having an ITIN shows insurers you are a tax-paying resident, which can actually help your application — use it confidently when applying for coverage.

Can You Get Car Insurance Using an ITIN Number?

Yes — many major insurers accept an ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) in place of a Social Security Number when you apply for coverage.

An ITIN is issued by the IRS to people who need a tax ID but do not qualify for an SSN — including many immigrants, visa holders, and undocumented residents.

You do not need citizenship or permanent residency to use an ITIN for insurance. What matters most is your driving record, your vehicle, and your state of residence.

Learn more about ITINs directly from the IRS: IRS ITIN Information.

Will Sharing Your ITIN With an Insurer Put You at Risk With Immigration?

No. Insurance companies are not immigration enforcement agencies and do not report customer information to ICE or any federal immigration authority.

Your data is protected by federal law. The Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act requires insurers to keep your personal financial information private. See the FTC Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act page.

Buying car insurance is a financial transaction — not a government application. The insurer wants your premium payment, not your immigration status.

Industry conduct standards are overseen by the National Association of Insurance Commissioners (NAIC).

Which Major Insurers Accept an ITIN Instead of an SSN?

Several large insurers are known to accept ITINs, including State Farm, Progressive, GEICO, and Farmers. Policies vary by state and local agent, so always confirm before applying.

When you call or apply online, say: "I have an ITIN instead of an SSN." Most systems have a field for this or an agent can enter it manually.

Some regional and non-standard insurers specialize in covering immigrants and non-SSN applicants. Rates vary widely — get a quote from multiple companies to compare.

If one insurer rejects your ITIN, do not stop. Another insurer in your state will likely accept it. Requirements are not uniform across the industry.

What Else Do You Need Besides an ITIN to Get Insured?

You will need a valid driver license. Some states issue licenses to undocumented residents — check the NCSL list of states that allow this.

You will also need your vehicle information: make, model, year, and VIN. Proof of your address in the US is typically required as well.

If you have a foreign license and recently arrived, some insurers will accept it. Others require a US-issued license. Requirements vary by state — check your state DMV for guidance.

California residents can review AB 60 license rules at the CA DMV AB60 page.

Step-by-Step Guide

  • 1

    Get Your ITIN From the IRS

    Apply for an ITIN at irs.gov if you do not have one. This number replaces an SSN for tax and financial purposes, including car insurance.

  • 2

    Gather Your Documents

    Collect your ITIN letter, foreign or state-issued driver license, vehicle title, and proof of address before contacting any insurer.

  • 3

    Find ITIN-Friendly Insurers

    Contact insurers that accept ITIN instead of SSN. Not all do. Ask directly: does this company accept an ITIN for a quote and policy?

  • 4

    Request Quotes Using Your ITIN

    Give your ITIN where the application asks for an SSN. Rates vary by state, driving history, and vehicle. Get at least three quotes.

  • 5

    Review Your Coverage Options

    Choose at minimum the liability coverage required in your state. Ask about additional coverage if your vehicle has a loan or high value.

  • 6

    Purchase and Save Your Policy

    Once approved, pay your first premium and save your insurance card. Keep a digital copy on your phone in case of a traffic stop.

  • 7

    Know Your Privacy Rights

    Insurers cannot share your data with ICE. The Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act protects your information. Buying insurance is safe and confidential.

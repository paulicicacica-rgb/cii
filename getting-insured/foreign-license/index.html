Car Insurance With a Foreign Drivers License: What You Can Do Right Now

Worried that using a foreign license will flag you to immigration? It will not. Insurers are not ICE — they sell policies, they do not report customers.

🇺🇸 All 50 States All Immigration Statuses 🚗 Getting Insured
Quick Answer: You can legally buy car insurance in the US using a valid foreign drivers license. Most states allow it temporarily. Validity periods vary by state — check your state DMV for exact rules.
Important: If your foreign license expires before you obtain a US license, your insurer may deny any claim filed during that gap — renew or replace your license immediately.
💡 Pair your foreign license with an International Driving Permit to reduce the chance of an insurer rejecting your application due to language or format issues.

Can You Get Car Insurance in the US With a Foreign Driver's License?

Yes — most major US insurers will accept a valid foreign driver's license to get you covered right away.

Companies like GEICO, Progressive, State Farm, and USAA have insured drivers using foreign licenses for decades.

Your immigration status does not matter to insurers. They want your premium, not your documents. No insurer reports you to ICE.

Rates vary by insurer and state — get quotes from at least three companies to find the best option for you.

How Long Is Your Foreign License Valid for Insurance Purposes?

Most insurers accept a foreign license as long as it is unexpired and readable — typically printed in or translated to English.

An International Driving Permit paired with your foreign license strengthens your application and is accepted widely across all 50 states.

Once you become a permanent resident, most states require you to get a US license within 30 to 90 days — check your state DMV for the exact deadline.

Visit the NCSL state license guide to see what your state allows.

What Happens When Your Foreign License Expires?

If your foreign license expires, your insurer may cancel your policy or deny a claim — act before it expires, not after.

Renew through your home country consulate if possible, or apply for a US state license as soon as you are eligible.

Some states like California issue licenses regardless of immigration status under AB 60. See the California DMV AB 60 guide.

New York also has expanded eligibility. Review the NY DMV license requirements for full details.

Is Your Personal Information Safe When You Buy Insurance as an Immigrant?

Yes. Insurance companies are legally required to protect your data under the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act — they cannot share it with immigration authorities.

Buying car insurance does not flag your name with ICE, DHS, or any federal agency. Your data stays with the insurer.

Review your rights under the FTC Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act page if you want to understand exactly what protections apply to you.

The NAIC also regulates insurer conduct. Visit NAIC.org to file a complaint if you believe an insurer treated you unfairly.

Step-by-Step Guide

  • 1

    Locate your foreign license and translation

    Find your valid foreign license. If it is not in English, get a certified translation. Some insurers require this to verify your driving history.

  • 2

    Gather your additional documents

    Collect your passport, visa or immigration papers, and any ITIN or state ID. Insurers use these to verify identity, not to report you to immigration.

  • 3

    Check if your state accepts foreign licenses

    Some states require a local license quickly. Visit your state DMV site to learn the rules before shopping for insurance coverage.

  • 4

    Request quotes from multiple insurers

    Contact several insurers, including non-standard carriers. Rates vary widely for foreign license holders, so comparing quotes is essential to find fair pricing.

  • 5

    Ask about international driving record credit

    Some insurers accept your foreign driving history to lower your rate. Ask each insurer directly whether they will recognize your record from your home country.

  • 6

    Review your policy details carefully

    Confirm coverage start date, listed drivers, and that your foreign license number is recorded correctly. Errors on the policy can cause claim denials later.

  • 7

    Begin converting to a US state license

    Start the process of getting a local license as soon as you are eligible. This often improves your rates and simplifies future insurance applications.

💡 If you live in New York, Massachusetts, or Florida, contact your state DMV today about driver license options for foreign nationals, because insurance becomes much cheaper once you have a US license.

Can I drive in the US with a Brazilian driver license?

Yes, you can drive legally in most US states with a valid Brazilian CNH (Carteira Nacional de Habilitação) for up to 12 months. Your Brazilian license must be current and you should carry your passport as proof of identity.

However, to buy car insurance, most companies require a US driver license or state ID. This is the real barrier for Polish immigrants and other foreign nationals, not the license itself.

Some insurers will quote you with a foreign license, but approval depends on your state and whether you have an ITIN or SSN. Florida and Massachusetts have different rules, so check your specific state first.

Do I need a US driver license to get insured?

Not always, but most major insurers prefer it. Some regional carriers and specialty insurers will quote Brazilian license holders, especially if you have an ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) from the IRS.

Getting a US driver license first makes insurance shopping much easier and faster. Many states now allow you to apply without SSN or proof of legal status. Check your state DMV requirements at NCSL state license guide.

Polish immigrants in New York and New Jersey can apply for licenses with passport and proof of residency alone. Do not delay this step, as it unlocks more insurance options and lower rates.

What documents do I need to get quoted for insurance?

At minimum: your Brazilian driver license, passport, proof of residency (utility bill or lease), and a Vehicle Identification Number (VIN). An ITIN is very helpful but not always required.

Get an ITIN from the IRS at IRS.gov ITIN page if you plan to buy or insure a vehicle. It takes 2-4 weeks and costs nothing.

Do not worry about immigration questions. Insurance companies are regulated by state insurance departments, not immigration agencies. Your data is protected under the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act and cannot be shared with ICE.

Which insurers work with Brazilian license holders?

Regional carriers like Bristol West, National General, and some state Farm agents will quote you with a foreign license and ITIN. Rates vary widely, so get quotes from at least three companies.

In Massachusetts and Florida, check local independent agents who specialize in immigrant clients. They know which carriers accept Brazilian licenses and can speed up the process significantly.

Major insurers like GEICO, State Farm, and Progressive have inconsistent policies by location. Call their Spanish-language lines directly and ask if they quote with Brazilian CNH. Be honest about your situation, they cannot report you to immigration.
